NIBE Heat pump smart controller

In this project it is investigated if an algorithm based on electricity prices, weather forecasts and a heat pump’s efficiency, can decrease the price of heating with a heat pump. The aim of the project was to make heating more financially sustainable, during a period of prices increasing. The project is made in collaboration with a client, whose heat pump has been used to test the product. The final product consists of three parts; a pulse counter, a user interface, and a controller for the heat pump. The pulse counter measures the pulses coming from the heat pump’s energy meter, allowing for statistics to be made. The user interface provides the user an overview of the heat pump’s settings and energy consumption. The controller is controlling the heatpump and will be schedueling how the heating is done.

Drone Tracking

The project enables real-time tracking of drones using an Acconeer radar unit, providing precise monitoring of their positions and movements. It offers enhanced situational awareness and optimization for various drone applications.
